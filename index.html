How to Create a Live Website on Github

Step 1

Click on the plus sign on the right side of the wesbite, and select "New repository."

Step 2

After typing in your repository name, click on "Public" so your repository will be public to everyone, and also click on "Initialize this repository with a README."

Step 3

Click on "create a new file" to start working on your code, or click on "upload an existing file" to add the files you already have.

Drag or add the neccessary files you have, make sure "commit to the master branch" is selected, then click on Commit changes.

Step Four

The site will take you to your repository menu. This has all the neccessary elements of your website. Click on settings.

Scroll down until you reach "GitHub Pages," go to Source, and select "Master Branch."

Step Five

Right above sources, your website is displayed. The blue background on that header indicates your site is ready for publishing. Click on your website to make sure it's live.
